Folate (Vitamin B9) is a water-soluble B vitamin found naturally in leafy green vegetables, legumes, and citrus fruits. It is essential for DNA synthesis, cell division, and the methylation cycle, a process involved in mood regulation, cardiovascular health, and detoxification. Folate works closely with Vitamin B12 and is particularly important during pregnancy for preventing neural tube defects. Serum folate reflects recent dietary intake, while red blood cell (RBC) folate is a better indicator of longer-term stores.
Lifestyle Guidance
If elevated due to supplementation, reduce or cease supplemental folate. Ensure B12 is also sufficient to avoid masking deficiency.
Supplementation Support
Reduce supplemental folate if intake is excessive. No additional supplementation indicated.

Zinc is a trace mineral essential for immune function, wound healing, protein synthesis, DNA production, and hormone metabolism including testosterone. It also supports taste and smell, skin integrity, and antioxidant defence. Because zinc is not stored in the body in any significant amount, regular dietary intake is important. Plasma zinc is the most practical way to assess zinc status, although it can fluctuate with recent meals, stress, and inflammation. A fasting morning sample typically gives the most reliable result.
Lifestyle Guidance
Increase oysters, red meat, pumpkin seeds, and chickpeas. Avoid tea or coffee with high-zinc meals as tannins inhibit absorption. Ensure adequate overall protein intake.
Supplementation Support
Zinc picolinate 15 to 30 mg/day for 4 to 8 weeks. If using beyond 8 weeks, add 1 to 2 mg copper per 15 mg zinc to prevent copper depletion.
